# Brandon Zelma Land Growth Platform — Implementation Plan

## Context

Brandon Zelma is a U.S. Army veteran and Ohio **Land Pro** agent with **Buckeye Land Sales**. He has strong field credentials (Natural Resources / Hocking College, wildlife, timber, GPS/mapping) and boots-on-the-ground storytelling, but **no personal website**. His current footprint is brokerage pages, Zillow/Realtor profiles, Instagram, and YouTube — the same catalog model every rural agent uses.

**What “he uses a broker” means (plain English):**  
In Ohio, a real estate *salesperson* must work under a licensed *brokerage*. Buckeye Land Sales is that brokerage. Brandon does the deals; the brokerage provides legal umbrella, compliance, and often listing infrastructure. A personal site is allowed and smart — it must **name Buckeye Land Sales on every page**, with brokerage name **equal prominence** to Brandon’s name (Ohio OAC 1301:5-1-02). He is not “independent” online; he is a branded Land Pro *of* Buckeye.

**Why traditional land sites lose:**  
Land.com, LandWatch, Lands of America, and most brokerage sites are **search catalogs**: price, acres, county, phone form. They don’t help a buyer *feel* the land, understand soils/timber/access/hunt quality, or trust a specialist. They don’t own the relationship — portals do. That’s the gap.

| Decision | Choice |
|----------|--------|
| Relationship | Gift / favor — Brandon operates under Buckeye |
| Ambition | Full growth platform (not a brochure) |
| Geography | SE / rural Ohio first, then expand |
| Budget | Lean startup **$0–5k + time** |
| Brand identity | **Brandon-first personal brand** |
| Market story | **Balanced SE Ohio land** (Hunt · Farm · Homestead · Timber) |
| Naming | **Brandon Zelma Land** (primary) / Zelma Land Co. (short mark) |
| Buckeye treatment | **Safest strong brand** (see Brand system below) |
| Admin | **Full decision cockpit** — CRM + analytics + land market intel |
| Veteran biz dev | **Extensive VA/SBA + Ohio broker independence path** (truthful; no “skip license” claims) |

**Honest $1M math (so the product design matches reality):**  
“$1M/year” almost always means **gross commission income (GCI)**, not land volume.

- Example: if Brandon’s take-home split ≈ 2.5–3.5% of sale price after broker split, **$1M GCI ≈ $28–40M closed volume/year**.
- At ~$300k average deal → **~90–130 closed sides/year** for a solo agent — elite / near-impossible alone.
- **Credible path:** (1) dominate SE Ohio listings + buyers, (2) raise average deal size, (3) dual-side more deals, (4) by year 2–3 add team / referral machine so the *platform* produces GCI for a small land team.  
The site is the **owned demand engine**; $1M is a multi-year systems outcome, not a launch-day claim.

## Veteran Business Development (VA + path off broker dependency)

**Why this exists:** Brandon may not know the full stack of veteran entrepreneurship programs. The site should educate *and* give him an admin roadmap. This is a first-class product area, not a footer link.

### Critical truth (must be crystal clear in UI + copy)

> **VA and SBA programs can help him build and fund a veteran-owned business. They do not replace Ohio real estate license law.**  
> To practice **without working under another broker**, he must eventually hold an **Ohio broker license** (and typically open/register his own brokerage, trust account, E&O, etc.). Until then he remains a salesperson under Buckeye (or another brokerage).  
> Framing: **“Independence path”** = (1) grow production + brand now under Buckeye → (2) qualify for broker license → (3) open **veteran-owned brokerage** (e.g. Zelma Land / Brandon Zelma Land Realty) using VA/SBA business tools where eligible.

Never claim “VA lets you skip the broker” — that would be false and harmful.

### Public site: `/veterans` or `/business` (extensive)
Accessible, respectful, practical — for Brandon’s own use and as **SEO/trust content** (other veteran entrepreneurs + veteran land buyers).

**Sections to build:**

1. **Start here — eligibility reality check**  
   - Service-connected disability vs not (affects VR&E)  
   - Links to apply; “talk to a VRC / SBA VBOC” CTAs  
   - Disclaimer: not legal/tax/benefits advice; verify with VA/SBA

2. **VA: Veteran Readiness & Employment (VR&E / Chapter 31)**  
   - Overview of tracks; deep dive on **Self-Employment Track**  
   - What it can help with: business plan support, small-business training, guidance on resources, possible startup support **if approved** (eligibility-driven; not a guaranteed cash grant)  
   - How to apply; what to prepare (business concept, skills, barriers)  
   - Fit for land brokerage: training, equipment/tools case, marketing setup — *if* VRC approves self-employment goal

3. **GI Bill / education angles**  
   - Entrepreneurship training options under education benefits  
   - Licensing/certification test reimbursement concepts (broker education where applicable)  
   - Link-outs to VA education pages

4. **SBA veteran stack (often more immediately usable than “grants”)**  
   - **Boots to Business** (entrepreneurship education; Boots to Business Reboot for veterans)  
   - **SBA District Office / Veterans Business Outreach Center (VBOC)** counseling  
   - **SCORE** free mentoring  
   - Capital: 7(a), microloans, community lenders — loans ≠ free grants; underwriting required  
   - Contracting path later: **VetCert / VOSB / SDVOSB** if he ever sells *services to government* (less central for residential land sales, still useful for brand/credibility)

5. **“Grants” myth-busting**  
   - Most veteran “business money” is **loans, counseling, training, or VR&E case-by-case support** — not unlimited free grants  
   - How to spot grant scams targeting veterans  
   - Legitimate checklist: VA.gov, SBA.gov, state veteran business office — never pay upfront for “guaranteed VA grants”

6. **Ohio path to independent brokerage** (step-by-step)  
   - Salesperson under broker (current)  
   - Experience: active license **2 of last 5 years** + **transaction points** (Ohio formula — surface accurate checklist + link to Ohio Division of Real Estate)  
   - Broker pre-licensing education hours  
   - Exam → activate broker license → form entity → brokerage registration → trust account → insurance → policies  
   - **Business model choice:** solo broker vs hiring agents (team scale toward $1M GCI)  
   - When leaving Buckeye: professional transition, listing ownership rules, non-disparagement, client communication

7. **Veteran-owned company operating system**  
   - Entity (LLC), EIN, banking, bookkeeping, estimated taxes  
   - Branding as **veteran-owned** (truthful; no false SDVOSB claims)  
   - Insurance (E&O, general liability)  
   - Tech stack he already has (this platform) as owned asset  
   - Simple financial model: split today vs 100% under own brokerage (illustrative calculator)

8. **Resource directory** (curated links, updated quarterly)  
   - VA VR&E self-employment  
   - SBA veteran resources / Boots to Business  
   - Ohio REALTORS / Division of Real Estate broker requirements  
   - Local VBOC / SCORE chapters  
   - Optional: Ohio veteran business programs (state-level)

9. **Downloadable pack** (lead magnet + real utility)  
   - *Veteran Land Pro Independence Checklist* (PDF)  
   - *Broker Path Timeline* worksheet  
   - *VR&E Self-Employment conversation prep* one-pager  

### Admin: Business Development tracker

- Document vault (upload business plan draft, certificates, education transcripts)
